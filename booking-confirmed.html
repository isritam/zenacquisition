Prepare for the Call

We take these calls seriously. So should you. A few non-negotiables before we connect:

  • Be in a quiet room — not a car, airport, or public space. We will end the call if the environment is too noisy.
  • Have your laptop or computer open. The call runs on Google Meet — phone-only won't work.
  • If you're in a partnership, every decision-maker must be on the call. No exceptions.
  • Block out extra time. Calls sometimes run over — don't schedule anything immediately after.
  • Be punctual. More than 5 minutes late means the call is cancelled and rescheduling will not be possible.
  • Have your questions ready. This is your time — use it well.

FAQ

Common
Questions.

What do I need to prepare before the call? +

Watch the video above, be in a quiet space with your laptop ready, and have your questions prepared. Think about your current monthly revenue, your offer, and where your lead flow is breaking down. That context makes the call 10x more useful.

How quickly do we start seeing results? +

Most clients start seeing qualified calls within the first 7–10 business days. The full system ramps up over 30 days, with weeks two and three typically being the strongest.

What do I need to have ready before starting? +

A proven offer that has closed at least one paying client, a calendar link, and 30–60 minutes for our onboarding call. We handle everything else — copy, targeting, sequences, execution.

Do you run ads? +

No. We run a fully organic outbound system — cold email, LinkedIn, and calling. No ad spend required. That's what makes the results predictable and the ROI clean.

What industries do you work with? +

Primarily coaches and agency owners in India — business coaching, marketing agencies, consulting, and professional services. If you have a high-ticket offer, we can make it work.

What happens on the strategy call? +

30–45 minutes understanding your offer, pipeline, and goals. We'll map out a custom outbound strategy and tell you exactly what we'd do — whether you work with us or not.
